## 7 Things You Should Know About Tor

 This is an article that clears a lot of misconceptions regarding Tor and the usage of it.

### 1.Tor still works

Tor is still useful as NSA has stated that they have not been able to bypass the anonymity provided by Tor.
Tor has as of now not been cracked at a cryptographic level

### 2. Tor is Not Only Used by Criminals

People assume that Tor is only used for evil and malicious activity, which is not true
.Tor is used by activists and journalists to bypass censorship and to gain anonymity.
It is used also for military operations and communications.


### 3. Tor Does Not Have a Military Backdoor

Some people believe that Tor was created by the military.
Because of this, it is believed that military have a secret access.
This is not true, as it has been audited by cryptographers and professionals, who confirm that there is no backdoor.

### 4. No One in the US Has Been Prosecuted For Running a Tor Relay

There is suspicion and paranoia around the usage of Tor and running Tor relays.
One thing people do not know is that it is not confirmed that anyone has been prosecuted for doing so
It is debated whether it is illegal under US law.

### 5. Tor is Easy to Use

It is as simple as downloading it through another browser
There other softwares available to use to like 'tails' which help strengthen your anonymity and hide activity

### 6. Tor is Not as Slow as You Think
Tor is slower than the average internet connection, but Tor has been worked on a lot to improve its speed.
One way to speed up Tor is by using multiple relays.

### 7. Tor is Not Foolproof

Some believe that no matter what you do with Tor, you are safe..
This is without a doubt wrong, as your anonymity also depends on your actions

### Introduction

Tor is an internet browser modified from Firefox.
Tor hides the user's home IP address whithout the user not needing to configure or setup any settings.
It is one of the most effective, yet simplest software for anonymity.

### History and Intended Use of The Onion Router

Initially developed by us government in 2002.
Tor allows for users to access websites and content that may be blocked by oppressive governments, and communicate with specific individuals anonymously.
Tori is open for improvements by anyone with technical abilities for doing so.

### How The Onion Router Works

Tor redirects user's traffic through different random relays. 
The exit relay does not know about the traffic route of the previous ones, making encryption stronger.
Tor is known as 'The Onion Router' for its multiple layers of encryption

### Tracking Criminals Using TOR

It is not completely impossible to break Tor, as FBI had done once before.
The biggest weakness of the safety of Tor is the user himself.
most common goal of online investigations is finding the true ip address.
